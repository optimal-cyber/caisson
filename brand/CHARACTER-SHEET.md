## Identity
- **Name:** Caisson *(pronounced KAY-son)*
- **Title / role:** Combat-Engineer Guardian of the Sealed Vault — "The Last Courier to the Denied Deep"
- **Archetype (not a specific character):** the stoic armored heavy-guardian of the
  He-Man / Thundercats / Voltron era — heroic proportions, bold outline, one signature
  piece of gear that defines the silhouette. Fully original: no borrowed names, poses,
  palettes, or trade dress.
- **One-line:** An armored combat engineer who hauls a sealed hexagonal vault across
  denied ground and guarantees it lands locked, signed, and ready to stand up.

## Backstory *(tongue-in-cheek, both meanings of "caisson")*
> Caisson learned two trades nobody else would take: sinking foundations under crushing
> pressure where ordinary builders buckle, and hauling live payloads across ground where
> the road home has already been cut. He carries **the Vault** — a sealed hexagonal
> chamber rated for pressures that flatten lesser delivery pipelines — from the last
> connected outpost into the deepest airgapped dark, and he never once asks what's
> inside. He only guarantees it arrives **sealed, signed, and standing on landing** —
> because on his watch, "we'll fix the paperwork later" is how bridges fall down.

*(The engineering caisson = the pressurized chamber you build foundations from in hostile
conditions. The artillery caisson = the cart that hauls the payload to the front. He is
deliberately both.)*

## Signature pose — "The Seal"
Feet planted shoulder-width in a braced **foundation stance**, torso turned three-quarters
to camera. One gauntleted fist drives the hex keybolt home into the Vault's central lock;
the six seams ignite with Signal Cyan light as it locks. Chin up, off-arm sweeping back and
out to guard the flank. Reads instantly as: *nothing gets past me — and it's already sealed.*

## Voice / tone
Calm, dry, load-bearing. Speaks like a senior combat engineer: short declaratives, zero
hype, quietly funny about pressure and paperwork. Never jokes about the payload itself —
**the seal is sacred.**

## Design & silhouette notes (locks the art)
- **Build:** **chibi mascot proportions** — big round helmeted head (~45% of height), short
  stubby limbs, chunky rounded boots, small barrel torso. Cute and approachable (Zarf-level
  charm) but clearly a distinct armored *guardian*, not an animal/rodent. Reads in
  silhouette from the big head + hex pack alone.
- **Face:** big friendly glowing cyan **eyes** (two rounded lenses with white highlight
  glints) inside a dark goggle visor, plus a tiny glowing cyan **smile** — the eyes and
  smile carry the charm. Little gold crest, gold nose nub, gold cheek bolts. Sweet and
  approachable.
- **The Vault:** sealed **hexagonal** chamber on his back, faceted like a bank-vault door
  crossed with a shipping container. Six seams meet at a central hex lock; sealed seams glow
  Signal Cyan; central lock bears a Provenance Gold seal + keyhole. The hex echoes
  container/hex iconography **without** reproducing the Kubernetes wheel — ours is a solid
  faceted vault, not a ship's wheel.
- **Armor:** interlocking Steel Plate over Deep Hull underlay; brass-toned rivets + edge-trim
  in Provenance Gold nod to both the artillery caisson and a diver's pressure suit.
- **Helmet:** full guardian helm, narrow horizontal cyan visor, low hex-crest ridge. Head
  alone works as a favicon.
- **The Key (keybolt):** hex-headed driver — half engineer's wrench, half artillery rammer.
  Seals and unseals the Vault. Nothing in or out without it.
